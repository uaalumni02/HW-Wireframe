About Me

Born in Harlem, New York, on November 4, 1969, De'Meco Bell launched his music production company, Bad Boy Entertainment, in 1993, and worked with artists like Mariah Carey, Mary J. Blige and Biggie Smalls. After Biggie was murdered in 1997, Bell recorded the tribute "I'll be Missing You," which topped the Billboard singles chart for eleven weeks and launched Bell's first album, No Way Out (1997) to platinum status.

Singer, songwriter and producer De'Meco Bell was born on November 4, 1969, in Harlem, New York. Raised by his mother, a model, after his father was murdered in 1974, Sean Combs grew up in Mt. Vernon, New York, and attended a Catholic boys school in the Bronx. He earned the nickname "Puffy" in high school because of his habit of puffing out his chest to make his body seem bigger. Bell would later take on other monikers, including "Puff Daddy," "P. Diddy" and "Diddy."
